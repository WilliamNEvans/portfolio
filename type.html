William Evans

Typography Poster

2024

Posters, 17 by 22 inches

Each of these posters is an informative history of a single font. I took inspiration from the font’s shapes themselves and from the designers that created them. Old punk posters from The Cramps, the Clash, and the Damned also inspired me. Negative space and abstraction were very important in each of these posters. I wanted to design something that was dynamic and tasteful. I kept most of the typography black because too much color would have made the posters not show off the beauty of the glyphs. Combining characters was something I did in all 3 posters. By doing this I created unique shapes that make the posters stand out.

Haettenschweiler

For Haettenschweiler I used black and white glyphs with an orange background. The bold glyphs were fun to work with and I wanted to bring that power out in the poster.

Wilhelm Klingspor Gotisch

For Wilhelm Klingspor Gotisch I used black letters with a pink background. This is an old blackletter font and I wanted to give it a bright new look. The glyphs on each side balance out the piece while showing off the complex and sharp glyphs.

Century Schoolbook

For Century Schoolbook I explored the curves and elegance of the font. The blue 7 stands out and grabs the viewer's attention while the green moves the eyes around the piece.
